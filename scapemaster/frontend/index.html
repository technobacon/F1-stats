    About

    How ScapeMaster works

    A daily Old School RuneScape stats game built on real, verified data — no trivia scraped from forums, no answers a model made up. Here's exactly how it's put together.

    The game

    Every question asks for a single number — a High Alchemy value, a boss's combat level, the XP to level 92 — and you're scored on how close you get, not just right or wrong. Three ways to play:

    • Daily Slayer Task — six questions, the same set for everyone, once a day. This is the ranked, competitive mode.
    • Training Grounds — endless single questions to warm up. Never recorded and never ranked, with a short delay after a wild guess so the bank can't be farmed for answers.
    • Duel Arena — endless "which is greater?" head-to-heads, played for a streak.

    How scoring works

    Each question is worth up to 5,000 XP. The score follows an exponential-decay curve on your percentage error: a perfect guess banks the full amount, and XP falls away the further off you are — gently at first, then steeply. Land within 10% and you've hit a purple — the raid-chest jackpot; within 25% is a green — solid loot.

    Your guess never leaves your device until you lock it in, and the answer is only revealed afterwards — scoring happens on the server, so HiScores totals can't be forged by the client.

    Where the data comes from

    Questions are generated from datasets built off the Old School RuneScape Wiki (quest facts, monster stats, release dates — available under CC BY-SA 3.0) and the wiki's real-time prices API (Grand Exchange prices, alch values, buy limits). Skill-XP answers come straight from the exact experience formula. The site ships a committed snapshot of that data, so it loads instantly and works with no live API calls; GE prices reflect the latest weekly snapshot, not the live market.

    The mix leans toward content today's players know — recent OSRS originals first, the beloved 2005–2007 backports next — with the classic era turning up now and then.

    How every answer is verified

    This is the part most trivia games skip. No answer is hand-written or taken on trust. Before a question can ship, the answer is recomputed from the underlying datasets (or the XP formula) by a validation step — if the computed number doesn't match, the question is thrown out. That gate is the whole point: it's what keeps a wrong or hallucinated stat from ever reaching you.

    Spotted something that still looks off? Every question can be flagged from the in-app data tool so it can be reviewed.

    Privacy

    ScapeMaster is guest-first: you can play straight away with everything kept in your browser's local storage — no account, no tracking that follows you around.

    • Accounts are optional. If you create one, passwords are stored salted and hashed (PBKDF2), never in the clear.
    • Email is optional and only used for the streak reminders you opt into — nothing else.
    • Analytics are pseudonymous and aggregate. They measure how the game is used in total and never feed your score or HiScores position.
    • Reminders are local. The streak nudge runs in your browser; there is no push server collecting subscriptions.

    Contact & feedback

    ScapeMaster is a fan-made project, not affiliated with Jagex. Found a bug, a wrong answer, or have an idea? The code and issue tracker live on GitHub — that's the best place to reach the project and to see what's changed.
