    About

    How GridMaster works

    A daily Formula 1 stats game built on real, verified data. No trivia scraped from forums, no answers a model made up. Here's exactly how it's put together.

    The game

    Every question asks for a single number (career wins, pole positions, points in a season) and you're scored on how close you get, not just right or wrong. Three ways to play:

    • Daily Challenge: six questions, the same set for everyone, once a day. This is the ranked, competitive mode.
    • Free Practice: endless single questions to warm up. Never recorded and never ranked, with a short delay after a wild guess so the bank can't be farmed for answers.
    • Arcade Over/Under: endless "who has more?" head-to-heads, played for a streak.

    How scoring works

    Each question is worth up to 5,000 points. The score follows an exponential-decay curve: a perfect guess banks the full amount, and points fall away the further off you are: gently at first, then steeply. Every kind of answer is judged on a fair yardstick: counts and points totals by percentage error, year answers by how many years off you are, and percentage answers by points of percentage.

    Get close and you've nailed a purple sector (within 10%, a year, or two points, by kind); a little further out is a green one (within 25%, three years, or five points).

    Your guess never leaves your device until you lock it in, and the answer is only revealed afterwards; scoring happens on the server, so leaderboard totals can't be forged by the client.

    Where the data comes from

    Questions are generated from the Jolpica/Ergast Formula 1 dataset, which covers every World Championship round from 1950 to today. The site ships a committed snapshot of that data, so it loads instantly and works with no live API calls.

    The mix leans modern, with more questions from the hybrid era (2014+), a deliberate boost for the Senna/Prost and Schumacher generations, and older eras turning up now and then.

    How every answer is verified

    This is the part most trivia games skip. No answer is hand-written or taken on trust. Before a question can ship, the answer is recomputed from the underlying race data by a validation step. If the computed number doesn't match, the question is thrown out. That gate is the whole point: it's what keeps a wrong or hallucinated stat from ever reaching you.

    Spotted something that still looks off? Every question can be flagged from the in-app data tool so it can be reviewed.

    Privacy

    GridMaster is guest-first: you can play straight away with everything kept in your browser's local storage; no account, no tracking that follows you around.

    • Accounts are optional. If you create one, passwords are stored salted and hashed (PBKDF2), never in the clear.
    • Email is optional and only used for the streak reminders you opt into, nothing else.
    • Analytics are pseudonymous and aggregate. They measure how the game is used in total and never feed your score or leaderboard position.
    • Reminders are local. The streak nudge runs in your browser; there is no push server collecting subscriptions.

    Contact & feedback

    GridMaster is a fan-made project, not affiliated with Formula 1. Found a bug, a wrong answer, or have an idea? The code and issue tracker live on GitHub. That's the best place to reach the project and to see what's changed.

    How close counts

    You guess a number. You're scored on how close you land, not just right or wrong.

    • Purple within 10%, the fastest sector
    • Green within 25%, still strong
    • Up to 5,000 points per question; six per Daily.
